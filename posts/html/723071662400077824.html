Pillows for Butterflies

The cloth sages of the academy were a strange and wonderful bunch. They lived in a world of their own, a world of fabric and thread and color. At the academy, they learned to sew anything, from the most delicate lace to the sturdiest burlap sack, creating out of cloth all sorts of beautiful things to further populate their world.

One day, two students named Willow and Roseo were working on a quilt castle project together. As they sewed with a lot of fun, they started chatting about butterflies.

“Oh, I love butterflies,” said Willow. “They’re so beautiful.”

“Me too,” said Roseo. “I think they’re so delicate and graceful.”

They talked for a while about butterflies, and then Willow had an idea.

“What if we made pillows for butterflies?” she asked.

“Pillows for butterflies?” Roseo tilted his head. “But butterflies don’t need pillows. They don’t even sleep in the normal sense.”

“I know,” said Willow. “But these friends who lead a turbulent existence need rest, and we are fantasy beings in a fantasy story. We should not be trapped by the rules of the non-fantasy realm. Let’s sew enchanted pillows that protect the butterflies so that they could roost in peace and recharge themselves with all the energy they need to frolic and drink nectar to their hearts’ content during their short time on Earth.”

Roseo thought about it for a moment. “You know, that’s a wonderful idea,” he said. “It would be a way to show our appreciation for butterflies.”

So Willow and Roseo decided to make pillows for butterflies. They went to the fabric store and bought some soft, colorful silks and velvets. They also bought some stuffing.

When they got back to the academy, they started designing and constructing prototypes of pillows for butterflies, taking into consideration the varied anatomies and vulnerabilities of the winged creatures. Soon, they had created pillows that were both aesthetically pleasing and functional, with the perfect amount of softness and support for butterflies.

When the pillows were finished, Willow and Roseo took them to the forest. They placed them on the flowers, and they waited. A small group of butterflies slowly flew over, curious about the pillows. They fluttered around them, admiring their bright colors and lovely shapes.

Finally, one of the butterflies landed on a pillow. It curled up on the pillow and became beautifully still.

“See,” said Willow. “They like them.”

Roseo smiled. “I told you they would,” he said.

Soon, a larger group of butterflies fluttered over to the pillows. They, too, landed on them and, it seemed, began to sleep, even though butterflies were not supposed to sleep like many animals do. The cloth sages smiled. They knew that they had done the right thing.

The butterflies loved their pillows. They would sleep on them all day long, and they would even take them with them when they flew.

The other students were amazed. They had never seen anything like it. They asked the duo how they had known that butterflies would love pillows.

Willow and Roseo smiled. “We didn’t know,” they said. “But we believed that they would.”

And that was the secret of the cloth sages. They believed in the magic of their creations, and that belief made their creations come to life.

The other students started to sew pillows for butterflies themselves, and soon the forest was filled with pillows of all colors and sizes. After they graduated, the cloth sages traveled to other lands and met new friends, who picked up from them the art of sewing pillows for butterflies. Their magic spread all over the world. Soon, there were pillows for butterflies in every forest, and the butterflies were all happy.

This is the magic that can happen when we believe in something beautiful.

Over time, fantasy realms faded away, leaving us with only the modern world we know. However, some of the pillows are still lying around in forests, and they are said to bring good luck to those who find them.

The end.
